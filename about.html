Our Story

A Century of
Artistic Excellence

Founded in 1923, MUSE has been a beacon of cultural enlightenment for over a century, housing one of the world's most significant collections of modern and contemporary art.

From the hallowed halls of our founding collection to the boundary-pushing installations of today's most provocative artists, MUSE remains committed to fostering dialogue between art and humanity.

The Building

Architectural Heritage

The MUSE building itself is a masterpiece of Neoclassical architecture blended with striking modern additions. Designed by renowned architect Arthur C. Sterling in 1920, the original structure features imposing marble columns and a grand vaulted atrium.

In 2015, the critically acclaimed "Glass Wing" was added, seamlessly merging the historic masonry with contemporary transparency, symbolizing the museum's bridge between classical foundations and modern artistry.

Preservation

Conservation &
Research

Protecting our cultural heritage for future generations is at the heart of the MUSE mission.

Our state-of-the-art conservation laboratory employs a team of world-class scientists and restorers. They utilize advanced imaging, chemical analysis, and meticulous restoration techniques to preserve delicate canvases, stabilize ancient sculptures, and digitize fading photography. Through our open-lab initiatives, visitors can witness this delicate preservation process firsthand.

Education & Community

Inspiring Tomorrow

MUSE provides free educational programs, workshops, and guided tours to over 500 local public schools, ensuring that the next generation has access to the transformative power of art.

Our residency program provides studio space, funding, and mentorship to emerging artists from underrepresented communities, giving them a platform to showcase their work globally.
